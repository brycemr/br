Career

My career aspirations are to work in a field where I can contribute to innovations that positively affect individuals. I feel a draw towards the fields of biomedical engineering and renewable energy as I see growing needs for clean energy sources and access to medicine in the world around me.

I currently work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Biorobotics Lab at Carnegie Mellon Univresity. In this position I am leading the work on a system to autonomously sort components that have been removed from smart devices.

Education

I am pursuing a Master's Degree in Mechanical Engineering - Research at Carnegie Mellon University from August 2023 - May 2025.

I graduated with a Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering from Brigham Young University in April 2023, where I also received a minor in Computer Science. While at BYU, I participated in the FLOW Lab, where I worked on offshore windfarm optimization using stochastic gradient descent.
