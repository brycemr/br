Collegiate Wind Competition

This project is ongoing, we will compete in May of 2023.

Objective

I am currently working with a team of 13 students to design a wind turbine to compete in the Collegiate Wind Competition hosted by the Department of Energy. I am working with a subteam of four students to design and create the control system for our turbine. The control system must be able to adjust the pitch of the turbine blades, slow the turbine to 10% of its maximum speed under emergency stop conditions, detect the ambient wind temperature, and adjust a resistor load connected to the generator. Meeting all of these objectives will allow us to regulate the power output of our turbine’s generator and score well in the upcoming competition.

Technical Details

We use an encoder to measure the RPM of our turbine, a pitot tube connected to a pressure sensor to monitor wind speed, and a voltage divider to monitor power output and load connection. The data acquired is passed to an Arduino microcontroller and used to regulate and update the states guiding the control of the turbine and load side components. To regulate the power curve, we designed a variable load that incorporates a resistor array to maximize power generation at wind speeds from 5-11 m/s. A variable pitch system will ensure steady power from 11 m/s to 14 m/s and will be controlled by a linear actuator connected to an Arduino. We have already built the pitch system and are currently prototyping the variable load design and testing the logic and functionality of our state machine.

Results

At the beginning of each month, we conduct a mock competition to check the current state of our turbine and estimate the score we would receive in the real competition. We have access to our university’s team results from the previous year, as well as the scores received by the other universities that attended the competition last year. We use these scores as benchmarks to help gauge the success of our turbine prototype. Our current prototype has already improved upon the score our university’s team received last year, and is able to achieve a coefficient of power near that of the team that won the competition last year. In wind speeds of 11 m/s, our turbine is able to produce 19.8 W of power with a coefficient of power of 0.152.
